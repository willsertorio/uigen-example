About the bench

I bring old Italian espresso machines back to life. Faema, Gaggia, La Cimbali — the ones built when these machines were the centrepiece of a room. I take them apart down to the last gasket, fix what needs fixing, then rebuild and refinish each one so it looks and runs the way it did the day it left the factory, only better.

004 — The workshop

A one-man bench in Turin

A one-man workshop in Turin, restoring vintage Italian espresso machines one at a time, by hand.

I am drawn to the machines most people have given up on. The ones in pieces in a basement, or sitting dead behind a bar. Those are the ones worth saving.

I trained as a mechanic before coffee ever entered the picture, which is really where this comes from. To me a Faema from the sixties is no different to a good engine. It was built to last, and with the right hands it will.

I work on one machine at a time so each one gets the attention it deserves, and I do not hand a machine back until it pours the way it should and looks like something you would be proud to keep on the counter.
