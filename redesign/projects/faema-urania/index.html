Faema Urania

Full Restoration
Chrome

Project Details

Owner

Bar Centrale, Bologna

Release Date

September 2024

Services

Full restoration, Chrome replating, Boiler rebuild

Duration

40 days

Budget

5.400 EUR

Overview

A two group Faema Urania from the 1950s, a large and imposing column machine from the era when the espresso machine was meant to dominate the bar. This was the biggest job on the bench that year. It arrived complete but tired, having spent decades in storage after the bar it served closed down. A serious restoration for a serious machine.

Objective

Bar Centrale was building its identity around vintage character, and they wanted the Urania as the anchor of the whole room. That meant it had to look spectacular from across the room and hold up to full service behind the bar. With two groups and a large boiler, there was no shortcutting the mechanical side. Everything had to be right, because a machine this size running daily punishes anything left half done.

Process

This was a long job and I gave it the time it needed. The large boiler was stripped, descaled, pressure tested and fitted with new elements and safety valves. Both groups were fully rebuilt, with every seal, spring and gasket renewed. The chrome on a machine this size is a major undertaking, and the tall columns and large panels went out for replating before I reassembled and polished everything by hand. The wiring was completely renewed. I rebuilt the steam and water valves so they seal cleanly, and refinished the eagle and badges that crown the machine. Final tuning took several days of pulling shots and adjusting until both groups performed identically.

Impact

The Urania now towers over the counter at Bar Centrale, exactly the statement they were after, and it earns its place by working flawlessly through a full day of service. Both groups pour evenly, the steam is strong, and the chrome stops people in the doorway. A machine of this scale is rare, and returning one to active duty rather than retirement was a genuine privilege.
