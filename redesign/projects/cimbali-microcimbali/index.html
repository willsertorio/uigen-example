La Cimbali Microcimbali

Compact Restoration
Repainting

Project Details

Owner

Elena Marchetti, Genoa

Release Date

November 2024

Services

Full restoration, Repainting, Electrical rewiring

Duration

18 days

Budget

2.200 EUR

Overview

The Microcimbali, nicknamed the baby Cimbali, is a small single group lever machine from the late 1950s designed for tiny bars and homes. This one came from a family who had inherited it and wanted it working again for the kitchen of their home rather than a commercial setting. Small machine, but every bit as much work as a big one.

Objective

The family did not want a perfect showpiece. They wanted the machine their grandfather had used brought back to honest working order, with its character intact. A few honourable scars were to be kept. The priority was safety and reliability, since it would be used daily at home, and a finish that looked cared for rather than factory fresh.

Process

Being a home machine that had been run hard, the wiring was the first concern, and I replaced it entirely to modern safety standards while keeping the original switch and look. The small boiler was descaled and resealed, and I rebuilt the compact lever group with new seals. The body had surface rust under flaking paint, so I took it back to bare metal, treated it, and repainted it in the original cream, deliberately stopping short of a mirror finish so it still read as a machine with a history. I kept the original badge, just cleaned, rather than replacing it.

Impact

The Microcimbali is back on the family's kitchen counter, pulling the morning espresso just as it did decades ago. Keeping a few marks was the right call. It looks like what it is, a loved machine that has been carefully brought back rather than erased. These small domestic restorations are some of the most satisfying, because the machine goes straight back into daily life.
